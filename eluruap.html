Work History of Eluru

Carpet Weaving Hub

Since the 16th century, Eluru has been world-renowned for its hand-knotted woolen carpets. Persian influence brought artisans who built an export economy that still thrives today.

The Paddy Center

Located in the fertile Krishna-Godavari basin, Eluru emerged as a massive trade center for paddy and agriculture, forming the backbone of the district's employment for decades.

Commercial Trade

Strategically placed on the National Highway and Railway lines, Eluru evolved from a transit town to a major commercial trade hub for incense sticks and palm oil processing.

Job History & Evolution

1980s: Agrarian Focus

90% of employment was linked to farming and traditional weaving. Government jobs were limited to railway and district administration.

2000s: The Service Shift

Rise of retail and banking sectors. Private colleges and schools became major employers as Eluru transformed into an educational hub.

2025: Tech & Industry

Transition toward digital services, healthcare, and modern manufacturing. Significant shift from ancestral agriculture to professional private sector roles.
