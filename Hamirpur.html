Work History of Hamirpur

Situated at the confluence of the Yamuna and Betwa rivers, Hamirpur boasts a rich economic heritage closely tied to its geography. Historically, the district has been a vital agricultural hub, relying on the fertile soils of the Doab region.

For centuries, the local economy thrived on traditional farming, particularly the cultivation of wheat, gram (chickpea), and pulses. Beyond agriculture, Hamirpur has a history of river-based trade, forestry product gathering, and artisanal crafts that supported local communities long before modern industrialization.

Evolution of Employment

Over the decades, Hamirpur's employment landscape has witnessed a gradual shift. While agriculture remains the backbone of the rural economy, there has been a noticeable diversification into other sectors.

The post-independence era saw a rise in government employment, primarily in administration, education, and public works. In recent years, the private sector has begun to expand, with growth in retail trade, logistics, agro-based industries, and the sand mining sector, creating new avenues for the local workforce.
