Chronicles of Heritage

Work History of Una

The Traditional & Economic Roots

Historically known as an integral part of the ancient Jaswan Dun valley, District Una has always held a unique position as the financial gateway to Himachal Pradesh. Nestled along the fertile plains bordering Punjab, the traditional economy of the region was founded firmly upon flourishing agrarian trade and localized artisan professions.

For centuries, the local populace relied deeply on multi-cropping systems, generating substantial trade paths for wheat, maize, and sugarcane. Handloom weaving, woodcraft, and terracotta craftsmanship formed the secondary baseline of occupation, helping rural artisan clusters operate independent communal trade systems that sustained the region prior to contemporary industrialization.

The Evolution of Labor

Employment History

1970s - 1980s

The Agrarian Backbone & Civil Infrastructure

Following the official formation of Una as an independent district in 1972, employment was predominantly rooted in state department expansion and agriculture. Government public works schemes and farming cooperatives served as the foundational sources of stable livelihood.

1990s - 2000s

The Rise of Industrial Polgons & Manufacturing hubs

With strategic transport access to neighboring economic centers, the establishment of prime industrial areas like Mehatpur, Gagret, and Tahliwal triggered an structural shift. Thousands transitioned from seasonal farm labor to steady corporate plant operations and mechanical trades.

2010s - 2020s

Commercial Diversification & Institutional Growth

The expansion of modern educational infrastructure, state health complexes, retail networks, and localized banking systems established a highly robust service-oriented corporate tier alongside traditional manufacturing pipelines.
