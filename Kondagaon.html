Work History of Kondagaon

Kondagaon has historically thrived through agriculture, forest-based livelihoods, handicrafts, and traditional tribal artistry. The district is known for bell metal craft, woodwork, and local trade routes connecting nearby regions of Bastar.

Traditional Agriculture

Rice cultivation, forest produce, and seasonal farming formed the backbone of Kondagaon’s rural economy for generations.

Artisan Heritage

The district is famous for tribal craftsmanship including terracotta, wrought iron art, and bamboo products.

Regional Trade

Local markets historically connected artisans and farmers with neighboring districts and tourism hubs.

Employment Evolution

Kondagaon’s employment landscape has evolved from agriculture-dominated livelihoods to modern sectors including education, healthcare, retail, and digital services.

1990s

Government employment and farming remained dominant with limited industrial development.

2005–2015

Retail expansion, education growth, and regional connectivity increased private employment opportunities.

Modern Era

Digital services, tourism, healthcare, logistics, and startups are now creating new opportunities for youth.
