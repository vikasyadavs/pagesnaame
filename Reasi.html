Work History of Reasi

Historical Governance

Seat of the Bhreyal State

Reasi boasts an elite administrative history. Established as an independent principality and fortified by Bhim Dev, its early economy revolved around military upkeep, localized trade, and traditional agrarian management.

Traditional Agrarian Systems

Terrace Cultivation & Orchards

Farming across hilly terrains sustained the regional workforce for centuries. Households heavily relied on maize, local paddy, and localized fruit orchards alongside small-scale animal husbandry networks.

Artisanal & Mineral Trading

Indigenous Handlooms & Refining

The district historically harbored small clusters of rural weavers and iron extraction artisans who utilized rich, accessible mineral deposits to fabricate tools for nearby regional trades.

Evolution of Employment

The Pre-Mata Vaishno Devi Board Era

Prior to structured tourism development, employment was highly decentralized, localized, and tied strictly to basic farming or seasonal pilgrimage facilitation in Katra.

The Institutionalization of Tourism

The creation of autonomous administrative boards transformed employment. Formal structural services, professional hospitality management, and public safety jobs scaled exponentially.

The Engineering Transformation

The 21st century redefined Reasi into a global infrastructural hub, drawing immense technical expertise for mega constructions like the iconic Chenab Rail Bridge and massive hydroelectric systems.
