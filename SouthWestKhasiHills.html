Work History of South West Khasi Hills

Agrarian Foundation

The economic baseline of South West Khasi Hills is rooted in micro-farming. Local growers mastered farming across difficult terrains, building self-sufficient systems centered on indigenous paddy varieties, high-altitude maize, and wild bay leaf harvesting.

Horticulture & Artisans

Famed for its unique microclimate, the region flourished through fruit cultivation, producing premium structural areca nut, local citrus, and wild black pepper. Simultaneously, village artisans utilized rich cane reserves for custom tool crafting.

Traditional Border Markets

Historically, the deep river valleys and footpaths heading south formed natural trade routes. Local producers gathered at border markets to trade mountain spices and forest produce for staple grains from nearby plains.

Job History & Employment Evolution

2012 - 2016

District Inception & Public Expansion

Formed in 2012 by separating from West Khasi Hills, early formal employment was tied directly to setting up state administrative modules, building infrastructure in Mawkyrwat, and expanding local civil services.

2017 - 2021

Eco-Tourism Development & Agro-Logistics

The discovery of unique natural sites like the Mawkyrwat Monoliths and scenic river viewpoints triggered a shift towards eco-tourism. Private jobs emerged in local hospitality, transport coordination, and regional spice distribution hubs.

2022 - 2026

The Digital Services Pivot

The current landscape reflects a transition toward decentralized private service structures. Employment is driven by regional banking networks, e-governance kiosks, private clinics, and structured cash-crop cold chains.
