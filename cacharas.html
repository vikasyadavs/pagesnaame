Historical Economic Roots

The Legacy of Barak Valley

Cachar has long been the economic heart of the Barak Valley. Historically, the region flourished through the Tea Industry, established during the British era, which remains a vital employer today. Beyond tea, the district served as a major trade hub between Northeast India and Myanmar.


Traditional industries like cane and bamboo crafts, along with extensive rice cultivation, formed the backbone of the local artisan economy for generations.

The Evolution of Employment

1980s - 1990s

Dominance of Agrarian roles and Government service. Silchar Paper Mill was a major industrial employer.

2000s - 2010s

Rise of the Retail sector and Private Education. Modern healthcare facilities began expanding in Silchar.

2020s - Future

Digital transformation. Focus on Logistics, IT services, and sustainable Agro-processing units.
