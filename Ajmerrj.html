Work History of Ajmer

Delve into the structural economic heritage that forged this tactical desert transit center across centuries.

Founded in the 7th Century AD by Raja Ajaypal Chauhan, Ajmer has uniquely operated as a focal point of geopolitical and trade importance in Rajasthan. Flanked by the protective Aravalli ranges, it became a highly sought-after hub connecting the fertile northern plains with the structural seaports of Western India.

Historically, the economic infrastructure thrived around structural craft networks, hand-loom textiles, and mineral mining. The presence of the holy Dargah of Khwaja Moinuddin Chishti and the proximity to the ancient sacred lake of Pushkar structurally engineered a robust service economy driven by hospitality, traditional logistics, and artistic trade fairs.

Agriculture within the district historically relied on localized stepwell ecosystems, cultivating distinct spices and rose essential oils that garnered trading accolades internationally.
