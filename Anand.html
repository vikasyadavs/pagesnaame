Work History of Anand

The Agrarian Foundation

Known as the fertile heart of the Charotar region, Anand's economic history is deeply rooted in tobacco, banana, and paddy cultivation. Its tobacco markets were legendary, serving as the primary trade engine for the district for centuries.

Birth of the White Revolution

The 1940s marked a historic shift when local farmers, led by Tribhuvandas Patel and Dr. Verghese Kurien, established the Kaira District Co-operative Milk Producers' Union (Amul). This transformed Anand from an agrarian town into a global dairy powerhouse.

Employment Evolution

1950s - 1990s: Cooperative Era

Employment was dominated by the cooperative movement. Thousands found roles in dairy farming, processing units, and the National Dairy Development Board (NDDB), cementing the "Milk Capital" identity.

2000s - Present: Industrial Expansion

The emergence of the Vitthal Udyognagar GIDC estate brought a shift toward engineering, glass, chemicals, and pharmaceuticals. Today, Anand balances its dairy legacy with a modern industrial workforce.

Present Landscape

Anand is currently seeing a "Talent Boom" in the Vitthal Udyognagar and Khambhat zones. The district is the highest producer of bananas in Gujarat, leading to massive demand in logistics, cold storage, and export management roles alongside the traditional dairy sector.
