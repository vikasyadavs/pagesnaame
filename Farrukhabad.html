Farrukhabad: A Legacy of Craft

Founded by Nawab Mohammad Khan Bangash in 1714, Farrukhabad has evolved from a historical garrison into a global center for block printing and Zardozi embroidery. Known as the "Potato Capital of India," its economy is a unique blend of delicate artistry and robust agricultural trade.

Textile Printing

For centuries, the district has been synonymous with "Chhipas" (hand-block printers) whose intricate designs reach markets in Europe and the Americas.

Zardozi Heritage

Farrukhabad is a primary hub for gold embroidery, employing thousands of skilled artisans who create bridal and luxury wear.

Agro-Exports

As a leading potato producer, the district offers extensive roles in cold storage management, food processing, and logistics.
