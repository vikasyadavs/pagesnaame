Work History of Jamnagar

From the Pearl of Kathiawar to Global Industrial Powerhouse

The Princely Era

Historically known as Nawanagar, the region thrived on pearl fishing and the famous 'Bandhani' (tie-dye) industry, supported by skilled local artisans.

Brass & Artisans

Mid-20th century saw Jamnagar becoming the 'Brass City of India' with thousands of small-scale units producing precision brass parts for the world.

Agricultural Roots

The fertile land surrounding the district continues to be a hub for groundnut, garlic, and dates, employing a significant rural workforce.

Employment Evolution

The last three decades have witnessed a paradigm shift from traditional craftsmanship to heavy industrial roles. While government employment in the Defense sector (Indian Air Force & Navy bases) remains a staple, the influx of Private Sector giants since the late 1990s has created a high-demand market for engineers, technicians, and logistics experts. Traditional Brass industries have modernized, keeping skilled labor relevant in a global market.
