Work History of Charaideo

A legacy built on royal roots and natural bounty.

The Ahom Era

As the first capital of the Ahom Kingdom established by Sukaphaa, Charaideo's early economy revolved around agriculture, metalworking, and royal administration. Artisans specialized in stone carving and weaving.

The Tea Revolution

During the British period, Charaideo transitioned into a major hub for tea cultivation. Sonari became a commercial center, connecting local tea estates to global trade routes.

Agrarian Strength

Post-independence, the district remained a leader in paddy cultivation and poultry. Traditional industries like handloom weaving continued to employ thousands of households.

Employment Evolution

Over the decades, employment in Charaideo has shifted from purely agrarian activities to a diverse mix of services. In the 1970s, government sector jobs were the ultimate career goal. However, with the modernization of tea estates and the rise of private education and healthcare, the private sector now accounts for over 60% of new employment opportunities in the district.
