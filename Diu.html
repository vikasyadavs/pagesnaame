Work History of Diu

A historical retrospective of how trade, location, and natural industry shaped the island district’s earliest workforce.

Pre-colonial Era

The Maritime Trading Hub

Diu served as a vital commercial outpost linking major maritime shipping routes across the Arabian Sea. The local population relied heavily on merchant trade, shipbuilding, and deep-sea fishing.

1535 – 1961

Portuguese Colonial Transition

Under Portuguese rule, fortifications and architectural infrastructure grew. The local economic landscape shifted towards defensive administrative roles, custom check-posts, and artisan crafts supporting the elite.

Post-1961 Integration

Fisheries & Local Distilleries

Following integration with India, mechanized fishing practices modernized the historic salt-pans and coastal marine infrastructure, establishing standard processing factories and organized retail trades.

Employment Evolution

How occupational focus transitioned over the last few decades from deep-sea labor to modern tertiary services.

Traditional Sector Decline

Manual salt-pan extraction and traditional wooden cargo vessel manufacturing experienced steep contractions due to automated, larger domestic deepwater alternatives across adjacent industrial coastal belts.

Public Sector Structural Shift

As a Union Territory component, administration and public services became primary career drivers for decades, introducing formal wage parameters, standardized benefits, and civic works occupations.

The Private Boom

The dawn of target hospitality policies transformed Diu from an isolated coastal enclave into an active leisure hospitality micro-market, fueling private entrepreneurship and continuous service-class absorption.
