Regional Legacy

Work History of Chamba

Agriculture & Orchards

Chamba has long relied on farming, horticulture, and mountain agriculture. Apple orchards, maize, pulses, and seasonal farming shaped the district’s rural employment patterns for generations.

Traditional Trade

Local markets and trade routes connected Chamba with neighboring Himalayan regions, creating opportunities in crafts, transport, and small-scale commercial activity.

Artisans & Handicrafts

Chamba is famous for its handwoven textiles, woodwork, and traditional embroidery. Skilled artisans contributed heavily to the district’s cultural and economic identity.

Employment Journey

Job History in Chamba

1980s

Government employment and agriculture dominated the local workforce, with schools and public offices providing stable jobs.

1990s

Tourism gradually expanded as improved transportation increased visitor access to Himachal Pradesh.

2000s

Retail businesses, hospitality services, and education institutes created diversified employment opportunities.

Today

Chamba is seeing growth in healthcare, digital services, eco-tourism, retail, logistics, and skill-based employment.
