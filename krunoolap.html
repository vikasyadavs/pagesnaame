Work History of Kurnool

Ancient Gateway

Historically known as the 'Gateway to the Rayalaseema', Kurnool thrived on the banks of the Tungabhadra river with strong roots in stone carving and textile weaving.

Agrarian Strength

The district has been a powerhouse for cotton, oilseeds, and pulses, supporting a vast network of local markets and processing units for centuries.

Industrial Evolution

The transition from traditional trade to limestone mining and cement production marked the mid-20th-century industrial boom in the region.

Employment Evolution

Over the last few decades, Kurnool has seen a significant shift from 70% reliance on agriculture to a diversified economy. While government sector jobs remain highly coveted, the private sector—led by manufacturing and services—now accounts for nearly 45% of the local workforce.
