Work History of Silvassa

Historically, Silvassa was a tranquil rural enclave predominantly supported by agriculture, forest-based livelihoods, and traditional artisanal crafts. The local tribal population relied deeply on farming pulses, paddy, and harvesting forest produce.

The economic trajectory transformed fundamentally after the end of Portuguese rule and the subsequent integration as a Union Territory. Major tax exemptions and industrial subsidies introduced in the late 20th century turned this quiet region into an economic powerhouse, acting as a crucial bridge between industrial clusters in Gujarat and Maharashtra.

The Evolution of Employment

1960s - 1980s

Primarily agrarian economy. Government institutional setups and community building served as the sole non-farm employment generator.

1990s

The dawn of industrialization. Substantial tax holidays catalyzed massive private sector investments in heavy manufacturing, textiles, and plastics.

2000s - 2010s

Exponential growth in factory operations. Silvassa developed into an international manufacturing hub, triggering a structural shift from government reliance to private technical employment.

2020s & Beyond

Modernization of the job landscape. Automation, organized logistics, and specialized engineering professions now dominate the corporate landscape.
