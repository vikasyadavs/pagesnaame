Work History of Eastern West Khasi Hills

Traditional Agrarian Focus

The historical economic layout of the Eastern West Khasi Hills plateau is anchored on high-altitude farming. Rural communities optimized step-farming patterns for producing local paddy varieties, sweet potatoes, and organic maize.

Khasi Handloom Artisans

Cane craft and hand-loomed cloth structures defined ancient micro-economies. Traditional tools, woven baskets, and field utility apparatuses were produced locally and traded down hillsides.

Mairang Trading Hubs

Centering around historical routes connecting inner community lines to state markets, seasonal market days in Mairang grew early barter links into organized supply spots for regional produce.

Employment Evolution

1995–2005

Primary Sector Concentration

Livelihoods centered entirely around self-sustaining crop distribution, community agrarian jobs, and entry-level sub-divisional administrative roles.

2006–2015

Institutional Building & Growth

Expansion of educational institutes, public road networks, and state social service programs shifted workers toward formal services and basic logistics channels.

2016–2021

Decentralization Dynamic

The preparation and eventual structural declaration of Eastern West Khasi Hills as a distinct district significantly expanded avenues for administrative careers and local enterprise setups.

2022–2026

The Modern Decentralized Era

Accelerating shift towards organized healthcare delivery, institutional education facilities, digital governance interfaces, and value-added agro-industries.
