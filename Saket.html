Heritage & Work History

Understanding the transition of Saket from its historic agricultural roots in South Delhi to a thriving modern socio-economic center.

The Agrarian Foundations

Historically situated within the southern reaches of Delhi, the land that constitutes modern-day Saket originally consisted of pastoral fields, traditional farming hamlets, and royal hunting reserves dating back to the Tomar and Chauhan dynasties.

Local communities were fundamentally rooted in agrarian operations, small-scale trade, and craftsmanship, leveraging the proximity to historic water networks and arterial paths connecting historic Delhi forts.

The Urban Renaissance

The transition began rapidly mid-to-late 20th century as the Delhi Development Authority (DDA) envisioned Saket as a premium, highly structured residential and commercial district. Traditional agrarian lifestyles gave way to administrative planning, bringing builders, architects, and systematic trade enterprises into the region.

Employment Evolution

How the labor demands and employment ecosystems within Saket modified across different decades.

1970s - 1980s

Dominance of public sector employment, community-oriented planning administrative jobs, and structural growth. Local career profiles centered heavily around government provisioning, academic foundations, and localized retail trading.

1990s - 2000s

The post-liberalization wave repositioned Saket. Corporate complexes emerged along with premium banking institutions. Private sector career opportunities exploded across finance, real estate consulting, and specialized administrative services.

2010s - Present

Metamorphosis into a major lifestyle, healthcare, and retail job market hub. The setting up of massive premium commercial blocks and super-specialty hospitals turned Saket into one of South Delhi's premier commercial employment epicenters.
