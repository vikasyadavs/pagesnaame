Work History of Kollam

Uncovering the ancient global trade networks and legendary cashew industries of Quilon

Kollam, historically known as Quilon, stands as one of the oldest operational ports on the Malabar Coast. For over a millennium, it served as an international merchant capital, drawing regular trade missions from Phoenicians, Romans, Arabs, and Chinese dynastic fleets. Its economic lineage was forged in the export of valuable local spices, black pepper, and high-grade timber.

In more recent centuries, Kollam achieved global dominance as the "Cashew Capital of the World". The establishment of extensive processing factories across the district created massive industrial employment, heavily mobilizing a skilled rural workforce—particularly women. Concurrently, its access to the marine-rich Ashtamudi Lake and the Arabian Sea turned commercial fishing and coir weaving into fundamental structural livelihoods.

Employment Evolution

Tracking the macroeconomic occupational transitions of the district

1970s

Industrial & Agro-Processing Peak

Widespread primary employment within hundreds of cashew processing plants, tile manufacturing units, clay extraction sites, and traditional coir processing networks.

2000s

The Corporate & Remittance Shift

A transition into organized retail banking, public administration infrastructure, healthcare setups, and massive workforce integration into foreign GCC technical roles.

2025+

Modern Tech & Marine Logistics

Accelerated corporate employment through advanced marine food exports, modernized heavy manufacturing, lakeside hospitality setups, and emerging digital service firms.
