Work History of Dwarka

A legacy built on spirituality, salt, and the sea.

Ancient Roots

Known for its maritime trade and artisanal crafts, Dwarka has historically been a hub for merchants crossing the Arabian Sea.

Industrial Dawn

The 20th century saw the rise of the chemical and salt industry, with huge pans contributing significantly to India's salt production.

The Artisan Era

Local brass parts manufacturing and traditional textiles flourished in the surrounding villages, creating a unique local economy.

Evolution of Employment

Over the last few decades, Devbhoomi Dwarka has shifted from a primarily agrarian and pilgrimage-based economy to an industrial powerhouse. While government jobs in the railways and administration remain highly sought after, the private sector—led by heavy industry and green energy—has become the primary employer for the modern workforce.
