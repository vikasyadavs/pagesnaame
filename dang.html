Heritage of Labor

The economic roots of the Dang District

Forestry & Timber

Historically, Dang's economy revolved around its lush teak and bamboo forests. The local tribes have been the custodians of the timber trade for centuries.

Tribal Artisans

From Warli painting to bamboo craft, the skilled hands of Dang have sustained a micro-economy of traditional art and trade long before modernization.

Agricultural Roots

Rain-fed agriculture, specifically Ragi (Nagli) and paddy, has been the backbone of local sustenance and rural employment for generations.

Employment Evolution

How the landscape changed over decades

1980s-90s: Gov Hub

Employment was largely focused on District Administration (Ahwa) and Forest Department roles, with limited private sector presence.

2000s: Tourism Boom

The development of Saputara as a premier hill station triggered a shift toward hospitality and service sector jobs.

2020s: Digital Shift

Improved connectivity has introduced remote work, eco-tourism startups, and modern retail chains into the district.
