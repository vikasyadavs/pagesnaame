Work History of Morbi

Morbi is globally known as India’s ceramic capital, with a legacy of royal enterprise and industrial innovation.

Royal Era & Craftsmanship

Under the princely state of Morbi, clock-making, metal crafts, and stone carving thrived. Mani Mandir and Suspension Bridge reflect engineering skills.

Birth of Ceramic Industry

1930s onwards, local entrepreneurs started wall clock and roofing tile units. Machhu river water and clay deposits enabled ceramic growth.

1979 Dam Break & Rebuild

Machhu Dam disaster destroyed industries. Morbi rebuilt stronger, becoming India’s largest ceramic tiles and sanitaryware cluster by 1990s.

Global Export Hub

Today 800+ ceramic units export to 140 countries. Morbi produces 90% of India’s ceramic tiles, employing 4+ lakh workers directly.

Job History: Evolution of Employment

From royal workshops to world’s largest ceramic cluster — how Morbi’s jobs transformed.

Pre-1990: Small Scale Dominant

Jobs mainly in roofing tiles, wall clocks, and agriculture. Family-owned units with local labor from Saurashtra region.

1990–2010: Ceramic Boom

Massive expansion in vitrified tiles. Technical jobs, machine operators, and migrant workforce from UP, Bihar, Odisha increased.

2010–2020: Automation & Design

Italian tech adoption created demand for engineers, designers, QC experts. Marketing and export jobs grew rapidly.

2020–Today: Diversification

Beyond ceramics: plastics, textiles, packaging units rising. E-commerce, logistics, and service sector jobs entering Morbi.
