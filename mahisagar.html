Economic Legacy

Tracing the roots of commerce and craft in the Mahisagar region.

Heritage & Trade

Formed from parts of Kheda and Panchmahal, Mahisagar has a rich history centered around the Mahi River. Traditionally, the economy was built on river-trade and forest produce.

Agrarian Excellence

The region has long been known for its agricultural resilience, specializing in maize, pulses, and oilseeds, which fueled the early market yards and trading hubs.

Artisanal Roots

Local artisans in Lunawada and Balasinor have historically contributed to small-scale industries, including handloom weaving and traditional woodwork.

The Shift in Employment

How the workforce has evolved over the decades.

1980s - 2000s

Predominantly agrarian. 85% of the population relied on seasonal farming and local government administrative roles in the old districts.

2010s: Industrialisation

The formal formation of the district in 2013 sparked a surge in infrastructure projects and small manufacturing units near Lunawada.

Present Day

A significant shift toward the private sector, with growth in education, healthcare services, and retail logistics across the district.

Current Job Market

High-growth sectors currently hiring in Mahisagar.

Manufacturing 85%

SME units and processing plants are in high demand of floor managers and technicians.

Healthcare 70%

Expansion of private clinics and district hospitals creating roles for nursing and admin staff.

Education 90%

High demand for primary and secondary educators in both public and private institutes.
