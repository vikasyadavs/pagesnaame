The Economic Heritage

A legacy built on fertile soil and resilient hands.

Agrarian Roots

Known as the "Grain Bowl of Assam," Darrang's economy has historically centered around paddy, mustard, and jute cultivation, supporting generations of local families.

Tea Legacy

The district is home to lush tea estates that have played a pivotal role in trade history, exporting premium Assam tea to global markets.

Traditional Handlooms

Artisans in the Mangaldai region have kept the rich Bodo and Assamese weaving traditions alive, contributing significantly to rural self-employment.

Evolution of Employment

Over the last few decades, Darrang has transitioned from a purely agrarian society to a balanced economy. While government services and defense (given its strategic location) were once the primary goal, today's youth are increasingly entering the service and retail sectors.

The rise of Mangaldai as an urban hub has seen a surge in banking, logistics, and small-scale manufacturing jobs, marking a significant shift from traditional labor to specialized roles.
