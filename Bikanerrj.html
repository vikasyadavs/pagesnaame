Work History of Bikaner

Traditional Crafts & Artisans

Historically, Bikaner has been globally celebrated for its exceptional craftsmanship. From intricate Usta art (camel hide gold embossing) to traditional hand-loomed textiles and pottery, the region supported a rich matrix of independent artisan guilds that sustained its legacy for generations.

The Camel Trade Route

Positioned along strategic historic trade corridors, Bikaner flourished as an oasis marketplace. Camel caravans bridged transactions across the Thar desert, establishing early banking systems, merchant houses, and trade links for silks, spices, and wool.

Agrarian Foundations

Despite the arid topography, smart historical irrigation and traditional water-harvesting allowed Bikaner to become an agricultural cluster. Famous for dry crops, pulses, and animal husbandry, it built the baseline supply network for modern agro-industries.

Job History & Evolution

1970s - 1980s

The Rise of Agrarian & Public Sector Support

Employment focused primarily on core agricultural trades and administrative government employment following the construction expansion of the Indira Gandhi Canal network.

1990s - 2000s

The FMCG & Savoury Boom

A major structural pivot towards organized private manufacturing. Bikaner's famous snacks (Bhujia) and sweets transitioned from cottage operations to mechanized global industries, creating thousands of factory and supply operations.

2010s - 2020s

Infrastructure & Mineral Exploration

The industrial private sector rapidly expanded as ceramic manufacturing, clay mining exploration, and solar renewable infrastructure projects created heavy demand for technical skillsets.
