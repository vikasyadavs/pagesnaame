Work History of Patan

Patan’s economy is rooted in centuries of craftsmanship, trade, and agriculture that shaped modern employment.

11th–13th Century: Trade Capital

As capital of Solanki dynasty, Patan was a major inland trade hub connecting Gujarat ports to Rajasthan and Malwa. Textile and bead-making industries flourished.

Patola Weaving Legacy

Patan became globally famous for Patola sarees — double ikat silk textiles woven by Salvi community. This artisan economy supported thousands of families.

Agriculture & Canal Irrigation

Saraswati river basin and later canal systems enabled cotton, groundnut, and pulse farming. Agro-trading and ginning mills became key employers.

Post-Independence Industrialization

Small-scale ceramics, oil mills, and engineering workshops emerged around Patan city and Chanasma, creating first wave of industrial jobs.

Job History: Evolution of Employment

From royal artisans to modern service economy — how work changed in Patan district.

1960s–1980s: Government Dominant

Most formal jobs were in state departments, education, railways, and Patan Nagarpalika. Private sector limited to family-run trading and mills.

1990s: Dairy & Co-operatives

Growth of milk co-operatives under Gujarat model created rural employment. Banking and primary healthcare hiring expanded.

2000s–2015: Retail & Education Boom

Private colleges, English-medium schools, and retail chains entered Patan. Youth shift from agriculture to service jobs began.

2015–Today: Digital + MSME

IT services, logistics, e-commerce delivery, and food processing MSMEs now hire locally. Govt schemes push skill-based roles over clerical jobs.
